\section{Conclusion} \label{sec:conclusion}

The sophistication of natural organisms --- in many aspects still unmatched by human engineering --- strongly evidences the creative potential of evolutionary computation, and also lends it unique charisma.
% The notion of being seduced by this charisma is a reasonable criticism of evolutionary computation \citep{moore2023evolution,woodward2009evolution}.
The evolutionary metaphor in genetic algorithms and genetic programming has proven highly impactful, both in guiding research advances and also, more simply, in capturing research attention.
While important reasonable criticisms surround metaphor-driven algorithm development \citep{moore2023evolution,sorensen2013metaheuristics}, we argue compelling potential exists to address these criticisms and strengthen the rigor of evolutionary computation by instead leaning further into the evolution metaphor by exploiting the field's framing to engage analysis and theory constructs from evolutionary biology and allied fields.

In this review, we have highlighted notable examples of such work reaching into quantitative genetics, population genetics, ecology, evolvability, and evolutionary graph theory, which has already provided insight into evolutionary computation.
Such research, much of it very recent, reflects only first steps.
Important extensions remain to be fleshed out and connected to \textit{bona fide} real-world use cases.
Further, important lesser-explored opportunities exist for arbitrage;
Section \ref{sec:future} highlights promising directions including in inferential observation, replay analysis, phylogenetic network analysis, and coevolutionary theory.

Productive exchange between biology and evolutionary computation is a two-way street.
Already, digital organisms find use cases in experiments conducted in conjunction with \textit{in vivo} inquiry \citep{sanjun2007selection,wilke2001evolution,hindr2012new}, and as a testbed for bioinformatics pipelines \citep{daudey2024aevol,haller2023slim,moreno2025extending,TODO}.
