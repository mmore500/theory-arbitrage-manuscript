\begin{abstract}
Algorithmic operations in Evolutionary computation (EC) are typically framed through the metaphor of biological evolution, which lends the field a heuristic flavor.
We argue that a deeper, largely untapped value of bio-inspiration lies not in biological systems themselves but in the scientific theory and analytical methods that have grown up around them.
Because evolutionary algorithms instantiate the same fundamental process as natural evolution --- heritable variation under selection --- large bodies of theory from evolutionary biology and allied fields stands to be ``arbitraged'' to characterize and explain EC, not merely to extend or imagine new variants.
This survey reviews where such transfer has already proven fruitful, spanning evolvability, population structure, and diversity maintenance and measurement.
We then chart promising further directions, including inferential observability, coevolution, adaptive momentum, and recombination.
Within these topics, Evolutionary Computation has not only leveraged bioological theory, but on accouont of its unique tractability as a study ssystem, contributed  back in places in developing theory and experiments in evolutionary biology.
In sum, we highlight theory available through the evolutionary metaphor offers as a promising route to strengthen rigorous, explanatory foundation for evolutionary computation.
\end{abstract}
