\begin{abstract}
As a field, Evolutionary computation (EC) is deeply entwined with its heuristic metaphor to biological evolution.
This framing has provided an enduring well of inspiration and spawned for numerous bio-inspired embellishments --- some productive, and many less so.
Here, we argue that deeper value from bio-inspiration lies not in biological systems themselves, but in the fields of science they have begat.
Because EC and natural evolution both instantiate the same fundamental process of heritable variation under selection, large bodies of theory and analytical methods from evolutionary biology and allied fields can be recruited to EC.
We review existing steps in this direction, by application of population genetics, ecology, biodiversity science, and evolutionary graph theory within EC.
We believe much untapped potential remains, however, and suggest promising opportunities in inferential observation, replay experiments, coevolution, adaptive momentum, and  phylogenetic network analysis.
% Notably, exchange between EC and bioscience has long been a two-way street; we also highlight instances of and opportunities for reciporical exchange.
Such interdisciplinary awareness and fluency will be key to advancing evolutionary computation towards its full potential, and broadening its impact through reciporical exchange.
\end{abstract}
