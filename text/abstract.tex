\begin{abstract}
As a field, Evolutionary computation (EC) is deeply entwined with its heuristic metaphor tor biological evolution.
This framing has provided an enduring well of inspiration for numerous proposed extensions and elaborations --- some worthwhile, and many less so.
Here, we argue that deeper, and largely untapped, value from bio-inspiration lies not in biological systems themselves, but in the scientific theory and analytical methods they have precipitated.
Because EC and natural evolution both instantiate the same fundamental process of heritable variation under selection, large bodies of methods and theory from evolutionary biology and allied fields can be recruited to EC.
We review existing applications of population genetics, ecology, biodiversity science, and evolutionary graph theory.
We also suggest promising opportunities in inferential observation, coevolution, adaptive momentum, and phylogenetic network analysis.
Notably, exchange between EC and bioscience has long been a two-way street;
we also highlight instances of and opportunities for reciporical exchange.
Better interdisciplinary awareness, and willingness to engage, will be an important step to advancing evolutionary computation towards its full potential.
\end{abstract}
