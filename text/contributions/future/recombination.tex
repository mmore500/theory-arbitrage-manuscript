\subsection{Phylogenetic Networks}
\label{sec:recombination}

The advantage that recombination confers for adaptation has long been recognized in both evolutionary biology and evolutionary computation \citep{felsenstein1976evolutionary,holland1975adaptation}.
In addition to crossover, biology also exhibits widespread horizontal gene transfer (HGT) between lineages (i.e., one-way via conjugation between neighboring cells, transduction via viral intermediaries, and transformation by uptake of environmental DNA) \citep{arnold2022horizontal} --- through transfers of individual genes, or even in clusters of up to 40 genes \citep{brinkmann2018horizontal,martin2018physiological}.
These processes have profoundly shaped evolution by extending regulatory networks \citep{price2008horizontal}, modifying metabolic pathways \citep{goyal2022horizontal}, and promoting modularity \citep{kreimer2008evolution}.
Despite its potential to benefit the constructive power of evolution \citep{wiser2019horizontal}, HGT has been explored but not yet gained widespread adoption in evolutionary computation \citep{goldbarg2009transgeneic,peralesgravan2008am,harvey2011microbial,mehrafsa2013high,tomko2013unconstrain,atkinson2020horizontal}.

Both of these phenomena create phylogenetic networks --- phylogeny structures that depart from a strict trie of lineage splitting to also have events where lineages combine or interact \citep{huson2005application}.
Such reticulate (non-tree-like DAG) biological history, requiring considerations and data structures beyond those found in phylogenetic trees.

In explorations of this topic, one important consideration will be being able to characterize the structure and consequences of lineage convergences; phylogenetic network literature defines several useful descriptors \citep{yu2011coalescent,solislemus2016inferring,choy2004computing,zhang2022weighting}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the number of reticulation nodes,
\item level, the extent to which reticulation cycles are isolated or overlap and interlock,
\item $\gamma$, the fractional size of each transfer (i.e., viewing distribution to distinguish few large transfers versus more frequent small transfers),
\item discordance among per-locus genealogies (e.g., quartet distance) which reflects the cumulative fraction of genomes routed through horizontal transfers, as opposed to vertical edges.
\end{enumerate}
Useful software and techniques for layout and visualization of reticulate networks also exist \citep{huson2011phylogenetic,solislemus2017phylonetworks,huson2025sketch,panssen2024phylox,schliep2026tanggle}.

Recobmiation, which also confers benefits for adaptation has long been recognized in both evolutionary biology and evolutionary computation \citep{felsenstein1976evolutionary,holland1975adaptation}, ultimately prodcues highly reticulated pedigrees called Ancestral Recombination Graphcs (ARGs) \citep{nielsen2024inference,lewanski2024era,griffiths1997ancestral} at the level of the individual that are structurally different \citep{godindubois2024apoget,kelleher2019inferring,wong2024args,jeffery2026tskit,}.
