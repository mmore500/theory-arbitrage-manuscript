\subsection{Best-effort Observation}
\label{sec:best-effort}

Modern biology is driven by innovations enhancing visibility into the natural world and greater data availability \citep{strasser2012data} --- taxonomic collections \citep{winsor2009taxonomy}, microscopy \citep{turner1890cell}, satellite imagery \citep{stark2016toward}, and high-throughput sequencing \citep{durack2019gut}.
Though data availability continues to grow \citep{sulston1983embryonic,sheth2017multiplex,weeks2023deep}, fundamental limitations exist in data completeness, particularly in natural history \citep{benton2011assessing,delsuc2005phylogenomics}.

In contrast to biology, evolutionary computation has, from the outset, enjoyed perfect fidelity and absolute completeness in data collection of diagnostic information necessary to troubleshoot, tune parameters, and develop theory \citep{hernandez2023suite,hooker1995testing,o2003digital,lenski2003evolutionary}.
% tailored to burgeoning deep learning workloads \citep{marcus2018deep,kaplan2020scaling}
However, powerful next-generation high-performance computing hardware \citep{zhang2016cambricon,emani2021accelerating,jia2019dissecting,medina2020habana,lauterbach2021path,vanessendelft2025record} is beginning to strain the perfect-observability paradigm.
For instance, to pack 880,000 processors onto a single chip, the Cerebras Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE) platform provides only 48kB memory per processor, requires multi-hop message routing, and exports data only from the chip periphery \citep{miyajima2025benchmarking,he2025wafer,vanessendelft2025system}.
Such constraints mirror broader trends in HPC where data storage and movement are becoming more expensive relative to compute \citep{buluc2021randomized,khan2021analysis,gholami2024ai,klasky2021data} and, in very large compute contexts, component failures threaten data integrity \citep{snir2014addressing,kokolis2025revisiting}.

These challenges in data storage and movement somewhat mirror costs and constraints faced in research studying real-world systems, which rely on best-effort observations instead of complete observations.
Such ``inferential observability'' approaches can degrade gracefully under constraints and interruptions \citep{moreno2027trust}.
% In the case of digital evolution, a key challenge is tracking population dynamics across a vast, highly-distributed fabric of memory-constrained processors.
Recent wafer-scale digital evolution experiments have adopted strategies inspired by real-world experiments that assume data collection is expensive, limited, and non-deterministic \citep{moreno2024trackable,moreno2025mutator}.
Phylogenies, in particular, can be richly informative of population- and organism-level dynamics \citep{genthon2023cell, levy2015quantitative,stadler2013recovering,moreno2025ecology,lewinsohn2023statedependent,nozoe2017inferring}.
Rather than exhaustively tracking parent-child relationships \citep{dolson2023phylotrackpy}, we adopt an approach inspired by bioengineered lineage tracking \citep{moreno2022hereditary} --- in which progressive DNA barcode inserts identify closely-related organisms by shared commonalities \citep{masuyama2019dna,wagner2020lineage,mckenna2016wholeorganism,kebschull2018cellular,askary2024lives,sashittal2023startle,kong2020celltagging,chow2021imaging,chen2022connecting,choi2022timeresolved,kalhor2016rapidly}.
We use analogous barcoding with single-bit markers, budgeting a 64-bit capacity per genome \citep{moreno2025testing}.
Once budgeted capacity fills, barcodes overwrite each other to maintain an approximate record \citep{moreno2024algorithms}.
% Barcode origin times can be inferred from storage position, allowing direct estimation of lineage divergence
% Barcodes remain chronologically attributed, allowing estimation of the point in time when lineages diverged from conflicts \citep{}.
Phylogenies incorporating multiple organisms can be constructed by agglomerative trie-building over shared barcode prefixes \citep{moreno2023toward}.
Such an approach scales to quadrillion-elapsed/billion-sampled taxa \citep{singhvi2025scalable} and tolerates data loss from device-to-host data corruption arising in deployed hardware \citep{moreno2027trust}.

For instance, even under serial processing, maintaining full records of genetic program instruction history under sexual recombination has proven to be a highly technically demanding, data-intensive task \citep{mcphee2016using,mcphee2017visualizaing}.
Such knockout trials --- in contrast to other aspects of digital evolution --- are notable in that combinatorial intractability has held back completely exhaustive analysis for all but the smallest genomes \citep{nitash2021information}.
Knockout assay experiments have, therefore, typically been limited to single-site \citep{adami2006digital}, all-pairs \citep{kumawat2023fluctuating}, or iterative approaches \citep{langdon2014improving,moreno2021case}.
Biologists offer screening- and sampling-based approaches \citep{amstrup2005handbook,mackenzie2017ccupancy,amsterdam2004identification,glanville2009precise} which can be used to approximate structural properties of evolved solutions without exhaustive work which can find use in evolutionary computation \citep{moreno2024cryptic}.
